What do Astronauts Do?

  A pilot astronaut works as both the Space Shuttle and International Space Station commander and pilot. During flight, the commander has onboard responsibility for the vehicle, crew, mission success, and safety of flight. The pilot assists the commander in controlling and operating the vehicle. In addition, the pilot may assist in the deployment and retrieval of satellites utilizing the remote manipulator system, in extravehicular activities, and in other payload operations. A mission specialist astronaut works with the commander and the pilot and has overall responsibility for coordinating operations in the following areas: systems, crew activity planning, consumables usage, and experiment/payload operations. Mission specialists are trained in the details of the onboard systems, as well as the operational characteristics, mission requirements/objectives, and supporting equipment/systems for each of the experiments conducted on their assigned missions. Mission specialists perform extravehicular activities (EVAs) and space walks, operate the remote manipulator system, and are responsible for payloads and specific experiment operations.

  Astronauts are employed by governments, educational institutions and a wide range of industrial establishments. An astronaut doesn't just get to float around space all day. Most astronauts spend very little time in space. They actually have so much to do in a day that all of their time must be tightly scheduled; much of this time is spent doing A LOT of training for many different tasks, such as operating complicated machines and completing simulated (fake) space missions to help prepare for real ones. They complete work as part of a team, but sometimes spend long periods of time away from family and friends. A lot of physical fitness is involved, and astronauts will work out for at least an hour every day, especially when in space, to keep their muscles strong.

Problem-Solving Skills

Astronaut use scientific observation and analysis to solve complex scientific questions. Creative Thinking may be needed to solve these complex scientific problems.

Communication Skills

Astronaut may write technical reports that may be published in scientific journals. They also write proposals for research funding.
