Overview

What do Aircraft Mechanics Do?

  To keep an airplane in peak operating condition, aircraft mechanics perform scheduled maintenance, make repairs, and complete inspections. They must follow detailed federal regulations that dictate maintenance schedules for a variety of different operations. Many mechanics are generalists and work on many different types of aircraft, such as jets, piston-driven airplanes, and helicopters. Others specialize in one section of a particular type of aircraft, such as the engine, hydraulics, or electrical system. In independent repair shops, mechanics usually inspect and repair many different types of aircraft.

  Aircraft mechanics work in hangars, in repair stations, or on airfields. They must meet strict deadlines while maintaining safety standards. Most aircraft mechanics work near major airports. Mechanics may work outside, on the airfield, or in climate-controlled shops and hangars.

Important Skills Used

Agility

Aircraft mechanics need to climb on airplanes, balance, and reach without falling.

Detail Oriented

Aircraft mechanics need to adjust airplane parts to exact specifications. For example, they often use precision tools to tighten wheel bolts to an exact tension.

Dexterity

Aircraft mechanics must possess dexterity to coordinate the movement of their fingers and hands to grasp, manipulate, or assemble parts.
